01

Tokenised Deposits

The programmable foundation for institutional blockchain. Mint, transfer, and extend fiat deposits as EVM-standard tokens within a sovereign Privacy Node, preserving the bank's balance sheet while unlocking new utility.

Why this architecture: Public chains cannot preserve the bank's deposit base. Permissioned chains lack programmable extensibility. Rayls mints deposits on a sovereign Privacy Node with embedded compliance, and optionally bridges to the Public Chain for yield and global liquidity.

Yield-Bearing Asset Vaults

Global distribution of institutional assets. Suitable investors earn yield. Global public chains as your secondary market.

Why this architecture: Permissioned chains limit distribution reach. Public-only chains expose private data and strategies. Rayls issues privately, then bridges to public vaults with onchain attestation-based investor access controls.

03

Cross-Border Payments and FX

A stablecoin sandwich from tokenised deposit to stablecoin back to tokenised deposit. Private cross-border settlement via Rayls Enygma in seconds, not days.

Why this architecture: Public chains lack payment privacy. Permissioned chains need intermediaries across jurisdictions. Rayls combines private deposit operations per bank with Enygma-encrypted public transfers, eliminating correspondent banks.

Private Tokenised Asset Trading

Multi-party atomic settlement between local institutions. Each party keeps full data sovereignty. No intermediaries. Governed by local regulation.

Why this architecture: Public chains expose positions. Single-institution chains cannot settle with others. Rayls Private Networks enable atomic DvP between sovereign nodes, with optional public chain distribution.
