Onchain FX and Cross-Border Payments

A local-currency tokenised deposit inside a sovereign Privacy Node, swapped onchain for a public-chain stablecoin (USDT or USDC) for the cross-border leg. Replace correspondent banking with transparent, privacy-preserving FX and settlement that clears in seconds.

The Problem

Multi-day settlement, opaque FX and trapped nostro capital

Cross-border payments route through 2 to 5 correspondent banks over 2 to 5 business days, with hidden FX markups and intermediary fees at every hop. Meanwhile banks pre-fund nostro accounts in every corridor, locking trillions in idle capital.

Status quo

Every cross-border payment carries a layered FX markup: correspondent banks quote exchange rates worse than the interbank mid-market rate, pocketing the difference alongside explicit fees.
Each payment passes through 2 to 5 correspondent banks, each deducting $25 to $75 per hop. Beneficiaries receive less than sent, with no upfront visibility of deductions.
Settlement takes 2 to 5 business days across time zones, weekends and holidays. Only 35% of retail cross-border payments credit within one hour (FSB/BIS, 2025).
Banks pre-fund nostro accounts in every currency corridor, locking capital at minimal return. Industry estimates indicate $27 trillion is tied up globally in pre-funded nostro and vostro accounts, with a 3 to 5% annual opportunity cost (Keyrock / industry research).

With Rayls

Option A — Local-currency conversion: a tokenised deposit in the bank's own currency (for example BRL) is swapped onchain via an AMM or FX contract for an international stablecoin (USDT or USDC), which is then used for the cross-border payment leg on the public chain.
Option B — USD-denominated institutional stablecoin: a USD stablecoin is issued for multi-institution transfers within a Private Network, then converted one-to-one with a public-chain stablecoin (USDT or USDC) for the cross-border leg. Private transfers stay inside the consortium; only the external leg hits the public chain.
Near-instant settlement, 24/7. The cross-border leg reaches deterministic finality in seconds, irrespective of time zones or banking hours.
On-demand stablecoin liquidity eliminates pre-funded nostro accounts. Capital freed for productive deployment.

The Solution

Onchain FX into public-chain stablecoin for cross-border settlement

Two deployment options share the same settlement layer: a local-currency tokenised deposit swapped onchain for a public-chain stablecoin, or a USD-denominated institutional stablecoin converted one-to-one with a public-chain stablecoin for the external leg.

Local Currency Tokenised Deposit

Client holds a tokenised deposit in the bank's own currency (for example BRL) within a sovereign Privacy Node. The deposit remains a bank liability on the balance sheet and never leaves the Privacy Node.

Onchain FX via AMM or Atomic Swap

The local-currency tokenised deposit is swapped onchain for a public-chain stablecoin (USDT or USDC), either via an automated market maker or via a Private Bridge atomic swap. Transparent pricing, no hidden spread.

USD-Denominated Institutional Stablecoin

Alternative path: a USD stablecoin is issued for multi-institution transfers within a Private Network, then converted 1:1 with a public-chain stablecoin (USDT or USDC) for the cross-border leg. Private transfers stay inside the consortium.

Auditable Compliance and Regulator Access

Operators can grant regulators auditor view keys or read-only access under local law. Transfers are fully traceable on-chain while private from uninvolved participants.
